How to Calculate Churn Rate

Monthly Churn Rate = (Customers Lost ÷ Customers at Start of Period) × 100

If you know your end-of-period count instead: Customers Lost = Start Customers − End Customers

Annual Churn Rate = 1 − (1 − Monthly Churn Rate)¹² — this compounds the monthly rate over a full year, giving a more accurate annual picture than simply multiplying by 12.

Retention Rate = 100% − Monthly Churn Rate

Revenue Impact: If you enter your MRR, we calculate the monthly and projected annual revenue lost to churn — helping you prioritise retention investments based on dollar value, not just percentages.

For Australian SaaS businesses, a monthly churn rate below 1% is considered healthy for B2B products. Membership organisations typically target below 1.5% monthly churn. Service retainers and agencies should aim for below 2% monthly to maintain sustainable growth.
